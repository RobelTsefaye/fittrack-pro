## Design Context

### Users
Serious intermediate gym-goers and consistent lifters who track performance data (weights, reps, trends). Not casual beginners, not hardcore competitive bodybuilders — disciplined athletes who care about progress. Primary usage context: gym floor, low-light, often one-handed on mobile. Secondary context: desktop review of training history and analytics.

### Brand Personality
**Clean. Disciplined. Performance-focused.**

The interface should feel like precision equipment — not a consumer wellness app, not a gamified social platform. Think: a high-quality piece of training gear. Confident and uncluttered.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark mode is primary. Light mode is secondary/optional.
- **Accent**: Warm amber/gold — `oklch(0.74 0.15 46)` — energy, performance, fire. Not neon. Not blue. Not orange. The amber of a well-worn gym, of iron under gymnasium lighting.
- **Charts**: Two-tone palette — amber (primary data) + steel blue (secondary data). Not grayscale.
- **Neutrals**: OKLCH, subtly tinted toward amber hue (chroma ≤ 0.006) for subconscious cohesion.
- **Typography**: Geist for body/UI. Barlow Condensed for large metric numerals (stat cards, big numbers) — athletic, precise, distinct.
- **References**: Strong (clean structure), Hevy (modern UI), Apple Fitness (premium animation feel).
- **Anti-references**: Gamified/badge-heavy, cluttered dashboards, anything beginner-oriented or consumer-wellness.

### Design Principles
1. **Precision over decoration** — every element earns its place. No gratuitous gradients, no decorative shadows, no icon-over-every-heading.
2. **Data clarity first** — numbers should be readable at a glance in a dim gym. Hierarchy via size + weight, not color noise.
3. **Amber is rare and intentional** — use the brand accent on achievement/performance moments (streak, PRs, active states, CTAs). Not on every element.
4. **Dark mode is the designed state** — light mode should work, but the considered, crafted experience is dark.
5. **Motion serves action** — transitions confirm state changes. No animation for decoration. Fast, responsive feedback.
